THE HEEMS STORY

From Humble Beginnings to Fashion Revolution

The Early Struggle

Yasir Himel grew up in extreme poverty in Galachipa, a small coastal town in Bangladesh. With limited resources but unlimited creativity, he began designing clothes from scrap fabrics at age 14.

"I used to watch the fishermen's nets and imagine them as dresses," Himel recalls. "The patterns of the waves, the colors of the sunset - these became my first inspirations."

The Breakthrough

After years of saving, Yasir opened a tiny workshop in 2018 with just one sewing machine. His innovative designs blending traditional Bangladeshi textiles with modern silhouettes quickly gained attention.

The "Monsoon Dreams" collection in 2019, featuring water-resistant fabrics inspired by Bangladesh's rainy season, put HEEMS on the fashion map.

Today's Vision

From that single sewing machine, HEEMS has grown into an internationally recognized brand while staying true to its roots. The company now employs over 50 local artisans from Galachipa, providing fair wages and skills training.

"Our mission is to prove that world-class fashion can come from anywhere," says Himel. "Every HEEMS piece tells the story of Bangladesh - its beauty, its resilience, and its incredible creative spirit."
