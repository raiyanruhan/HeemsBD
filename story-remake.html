THE TAINTED BOY

YASIR HIMEL

A Cursed Child

He was cursed at birth, though he doesn't know it.
Cursed not by a witch or wizard, but by the poison woven into his bones.
He cannot have what most of us take for granted:
the first true friend, the usual human comfort. Friendship, even love.
Such simple things for most, a mountain for him, stepped upon
slowly, painfully, or all at once.

Over the years, many have followed him out.
Most in anger... What he does was the simplest thing of all:
To finally let go
of something I can't hold
Even that I would not allow.
This is the story of a boy named Taint, sets off in search of an ending.
Finding himself on a strange, twisted, and lonely road.
a sorrow-twisted land of bickering gnomes, militant squirrels,
goblins who aren't quite goblins, and a monster who isn't evil.

This is not a tale of heroes.
It is a story of a boy who is sick and tired.
a twisted tale of memory, and the cruel kind of magic that doesn't
look like magic at all.

Taint

Once known as The Tainted Boy

No one remembers what his name was.
Maybe he never had one.
The villagers simply called him Taint, or the Tainted Boy
in a hushed tone, only, written in warnings.
TAINT. TAINT. TAINT.

Shorter. Sharper. Easier to forget.
And, eventually, they did forget.
Buildings burned, families cracked.
He fell so deep and so sad, eventually.
All of his cries died off.
Eventually, they stopped trying
to settle.

The Crone

Made of memory. Unraveled by it.

Deep in the forest, hidden beneath twisted roots and the hushed whisper of ancient oaks.
She has existed for what seems like forever.
She mutters to herself, to Taint, who may be listening.
but he hasn't been able to decipher the words
to escape the twisted, terrible
things that have been cracked.
She knows it isn't shimmer or shine.
She doesn't know it's a boy.

The Full Story

Taint was born under a cursed moon, though he didn't know it. The curse was not a magical spell, but a slow-acting poison that seeped into his very bones, making it impossible for him to form true connections. The things others took for granted—a warm hug from a friend, the simple joy of laughter with loved ones—were a painful, unreachable mountain for him. Anyone who drew too close to Taint would find themselves consumed by a deep, inexplicable anger. Friends would turn into enemies, and loved ones would flee, their hearts heavy with a sorrow that wasn't their own.

As the years passed, the village, once a bustling hub of life, grew quiet around him. Families left, their homes abandoned, their hearts hardened by the invisible poison Taint carried. He lived in the shadow of their warnings: "TAINT. TAINT. TAINT." The words were like a brand, and they followed him wherever he went. Buildings burned, friendships shattered, and his own cries for help went unheard. The constant loneliness and sadness eventually wore him down, silencing his pleas for connection until all that remained was a quiet despair. He stopped trying to mend what was broken and simply existed in the solitude he had been condemned to.

Driven by a need to find a place where he truly belonged, Taint set off on a lonely road. He sought not a heroic quest, but simply an end to his torment. He found himself in a strange, sorrow-twisted land filled with bickering gnomes, militant squirrels, and goblins who weren't quite goblins. It was a world that, like him, was broken in a unique way. He learned to navigate this new land, but the true test came when he stumbled upon the Crone. She was a creature of memory, unraveled by it, and her very existence was a mirror of Taint's own curse. She spoke in whispers of ancient things, and though Taint could not understand her words, he knew she was the key to his journey.

The Crone was hidden deep within the forest, in a grotto of twisted roots and ancient oaks. She had lived for what seems like an eternity, muttering to herself and to the boy who listened but couldn't understand. She saw not a boy, but a tapestry of fractured memories, a living story of sorrow. Her words were a twisted kind of magic that didn't look like magic at all—a truth that resonated deeply with Taint's own experiences. She knew the poison in his bones and understood the pain it caused, and through her, Taint finally began to see that his curse might not be an ending, but the beginning of a different kind of life—a life woven from the threads of memory and the quiet strength of solitude.
